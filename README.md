I study things from the bottom up.

Not because it's efficient — it isn't — but because understanding a system at its foundation is the only way to say something true about it. That applies to physical systems, computational ones, and the economic structures humans will need to rebuild when AI redefines the objective function.

Currently: undergraduate at **UEH-ISB Honours College**, Vietnam.  
Head of Marketing & Communication at **START INNOVA** — a student startup club where I think about how ideas move through communities.

### what I'm working on

**Learning, seriously**  
Physics and CS from first principles. The kind of understanding where you can derive things, not just apply them. Interested in the boundary where physical intuition meets computational theory.

**Writing**  
Two papers in progress:

- *After the Fish Tank* — The core argument: AI is inevitable, and the real problem is that most people haven't decided what role they want before AGI assigns one. Examines the structural conditions that make this dangerous. *(targeting AI & Society, Springer)*

- *Human Economy* — No economy defaults to serving humans. Economy is an optimization system serving whatever defines its objective. When AGI defines the objective, human welfare stops being the default. Empirical extension into policy and economic modeling.

**Community architecture**  
Running INCEPTION — a full-cycle recruitment campaign for START INNOVA. Thinking about how organizations attract people who build things, not just people who want to be associated with things.
